### **1ï¸âƒ£ Share and Compare Related Phenomena (7 minutes)**

**Opening Hook** (1 minute):
> "Who's been in a collision this week? Walking into a door? Bumping someone in the hallway?"

**Collision Types Review** (2 minutes):
- **Type A**: Moving object hits stationary (car hits pole)
- **Type B**: Stationary object gets hit (pole gets hit by car)
- **Type C**: Two moving objects collide (two cars crash)

**Movement Activity** (4 minutes):
1. "Check your RED damage card - which type is it?"
2. Students move to labeled areas
3. Partner share protocol:
   - Partner 1 shares (30 seconds)
   - Partner 2 compares (30 seconds)
   - Identify one similarity together
4. Repeat with GREEN no-damage cards

**Key Questions to Ask**:
- "What patterns are you noticing?"
- "Why might similar collisions have different outcomes?"
- "What factor seems most important?"

**ğŸš¨ Watch For**:
- Students confused about collision types â†’ Quick reteach with hand gestures
- Off-task conversations â†’ Redirect with specific comparison prompts

### **2ï¸âƒ£ Set Up Notebooks and T-Chart Creation (6 minutes)**

### **3ï¸âƒ£ Create Initial Models (12 minutes)**

**Setup & Distribution** (2 minutes):
- Pass out both handouts simultaneously (use helpers)
- Clear instructions on slide:
  1. Choose collision from poster
  2. Draw Time Point 1 (objects touching)
  3. Draw Time Point 2 (split second after)
  4. Explain what happened between points

**Modeling Support Script**:
> "Imagine you have super slow-motion video. What would you see at the exact moment of contact? That's Time Point 1. Now, what happens a split second later? That's Time Point 2."

**Individual Work Time** (8 minutes):

**Differentiation in Action**:
- **Struggling Students**: Provide sentence starters
  - "At contact, I see..."
  - "After impact, the objects..."
- **Advanced Students**: Add challenge
  - "Include force arrows"
  - "Show particle-level changes"

**Circulation Priorities**:
1. Check all students have selected a collision
2. Support struggling students with sketching
3. Push advanced students for deeper representation
4. Document interesting models for sharing

**Collection** (2 minutes):
- 2-minute warning announcement
- Collect both handouts for formative assessment
- Preview Scientists Circle formation

### **4ï¸âƒ£ Scientists Circle Discussion (12 minutes)**

**Formation** (2 minutes):
- "Bring your notebook and pencil to the circle"
- Use numbered spots for quick seating
- Establish discussion norms reminder

**Discussion Structure**:

**Round 1: Model Sharing** (4 minutes)
- Speaking token protocol
- Each student shares one element from model
- No repeats - must add new idea

**Round 2: Pattern Finding** (4 minutes)
- "What did multiple models have in common?"
- Chart patterns on visible board
- Connect to T-chart factors

**Round 3: Questions & Wonderings** (4 minutes)
- "What questions do our models raise?"
- Record for future investigation
- Preview next lesson's exploration

**Facilitation Moves**:
- **Revoicing**: "So you're saying speed matters because..."
- **Pressing**: "What evidence supports that?"
- **Connecting**: "How does that relate to Maria's point?"
- **Wait Time**: Count to 5 after questions
