### Model Sharing Protocol
**Time**: 8 minutes after individual model creation
```
PARTNER MODEL EXCHANGE

Step 1 (2 min): Partner A explains their model
Partner B listens and asks ONE clarifying question

Step 2 (2 min): Partner B explains their model
Partner A listens and asks ONE clarifying question

Step 3 (2 min): Find similarities
"Our models both show..."
"We both think..."

Step 4 (2 min): Find differences
"Your model shows... while mine shows..."
"I included... but you included..."

Step 5: Together, write one question both models leave unanswered
```

## PRACTICAL IMPLEMENTATION TIPS

### Time-Saving Strategies

**Prep Once, Use Multiple Times**:
- Laminate observation sheets for reuse
- Create template forms in Google Docs
- Develop signal systems students learn once
- Design general rubrics adaptable to multiple activities

**Efficient Data Collection**:
- Use smartphone camera to capture student work quickly
- Audio record student explanations during work time
- Take photos of T-charts before erasing for reference
- Use class lists with checkboxes for quick notation

### Student Training for Self-Assessment

**Week 1**: Introduce self-assessment language and expectations
**Week 2**: Model how to use rubrics and reflection questions
**Week 3**: Practice peer feedback with structured protocols
**Week 4**: Students begin choosing appropriate assessment tools

### Making Assessment Actionable

**Daily**: Review exit tickets to plan tomorrow's opening
**Weekly**: Analyze patterns to group students for support
**Unit**: Use formative evidence to prepare summative assessments
**Ongoing**: Celebrate growth and adjust instruction continuously
